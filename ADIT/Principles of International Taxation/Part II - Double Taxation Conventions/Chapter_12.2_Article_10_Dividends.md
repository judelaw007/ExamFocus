## 1. INTRODUCTION: THE ROLE OF ARTICLE 10

### 1.1 Purpose and Function

Article 10 allocates taxing rights over **dividend income** between the source state (where the paying company is resident) and the residence state (where the recipient is resident). Unlike Article 6 (immovable property), Article 10 uses a **"shared allocation"** approach with **limited source taxation**.

### 1.2 Nature of Dividends

```
DIVIDENDS AS PASSIVE INCOME
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

CHARACTERISTICS:
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ • PASSIVE INCOME: Recipient does not participate in the        │
│   activity generating the income (OECD Glossary)               │
│ • Cash flow obtained without continuous time involvement       │
│ • Investment income with lack of control over source           │
│                                                                 │
│ DISTINGUISHED FROM:                                             │
│ • Business profits (Article 7) - active income                 │
│ • Employment income (Article 15) - labour income               │
│ • Capital gains (Article 13) - realisation on disposal         │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

### 1.3 Policy Rationale: Shared Allocation

```
WHY "SHARED ALLOCATION" FOR DIVIDENDS?
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

OECD COMMENTARY [5] POSITION:
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ "Taxation of dividends EXCLUSIVELY in the state of source is   │
│ NOT acceptable as a general rule"                               │
│                                                                 │
│ "Taxation of dividends EXCLUSIVELY in the state of the         │
│ beneficiary's residence is NOT feasible as a general rule"     │
│                                                                 │
│ RESULT: Shared/compromise allocation                            │
│ → Source state: LIMITED right to tax (WHT capped)              │
│ → Residence state: UNLIMITED right to tax (must give credit)   │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

RATIONALE FOR SOURCE STATE RIGHT:
• Dividends derive from company profits earned in source state
• Source state provided infrastructure for profit generation
• Historical practice supports some source taxation

RATIONALE FOR LIMITATION:
• Avoid excessive double taxation
• Encourage cross-border investment
• Balance between source and residence claims
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
```

### 1.4 Historical Development

**EXAM RELEVANT** (June 2022 Q4 tested this history):

| Year | Development | Approach |
|------|-------------|----------|
| **1927** | League of Nations Report, Art. 4 | Unlimited source taxation (real centre of management) |
| **1943** | Mexico Draft, Art. IX | Unlimited source taxation (where capital invested) |
| **1946** | London Draft, Art. VIII | Exclusive residence for inter-company dividends ("dominant participation"); prohibition of extraterritorial taxation |
| **1963** | OECD MTC | Shared taxing rights with limited source taxation (current approach) |

## 2. ARTICLE 10 TEXT AND STRUCTURE (OECD MTC 2017)

### 2.1 Full Text

**Article 10(1):**
> "Dividends paid by a company which is a resident of a Contracting State to a resident of the other Contracting State may be taxed in that other State."

**Article 10(2):**
> "However, such dividends may also be taxed in the Contracting State of which the company paying the dividends is a resident and according to the laws of that State, but if the beneficial owner of the dividends is a resident of the other Contracting State, the tax so charged shall not exceed:
> (a) 5 per cent of the gross amount of the dividends if the beneficial owner is a company (other than a partnership) which holds directly at least 25 per cent of the capital of the company paying the dividends throughout a 365 day period that includes the day of the payment of the dividend...
> (b) 15 per cent of the gross amount of the dividends in all other cases."

**Article 10(3):** [Definition of "dividends"]

**Article 10(4):** [PE exception]

**Article 10(5):** [Prohibition of extraterritorial taxation]

## 3. ARTICLE 10(1) AND (2): THE TAXING RIGHTS ALLOCATION

### 3.1 Article 10(1): Residence State Right

```
RESIDENCE STATE TAXATION
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

"Dividends paid by a company which is a resident of a Contracting
State to a resident of the other Contracting State may be taxed
in that other State."

KEY ELEMENTS:
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ 1. PAYER: Company resident of one CS                            │
│    → Ensures bilateral application                              │
│    → Not dividends from third state companies                  │
│                                                                 │
│ 2. RECIPIENT: Resident of OTHER CS                              │
│    → Treaty applies only to residents                           │
│    → Residency under Article 4                                  │
│                                                                 │
│ 3. "MAY BE TAXED": Permission, not obligation                   │
│    → Residence state HAS THE RIGHT to tax                       │
│    → No limitation on residence state's taxation               │
│                                                                 │
│ 4. "PAYMENT": Very wide meaning (Commentary [7])                │
│    → Includes constructive payments                             │
│    → Domestic law determines what constitutes payment          │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
```

### 3.2 Article 10(2): Source State Right (Limited)

**EXAM CRITICAL**: The WHT rate limits and beneficial owner requirement

```
SOURCE STATE TAXATION - LIMITED RATES
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

OECD MTC RATES:
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ ARTICLE 10(2)(a): DIRECT INVESTMENT - 5% maximum               │
│ Requirements:                                                   │
│ • Beneficial owner is a COMPANY (not partnership)              │
│ • Holds DIRECTLY at least 25% of capital                        │
│ • Held throughout 365-day period including payment date        │
│                                                                 │
│ ARTICLE 10(2)(b): PORTFOLIO INVESTMENT - 15% maximum           │
│ • All other cases where beneficial owner requirement met       │

│ • Companies with <25% holding                                   │

└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

RATIONALE FOR LOWER RATE ON DIRECT INVESTMENT:
→ Direct investment contributes to economic development
→ Lower rate encourages foreign direct investment (FDI)
→ Inter-company dividends often subject to participation
   exemption domestically
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
```

### 3.3 Beneficial Owner Requirement

**EXAM CRITICAL**: The beneficial owner concept appears in Articles 10, 11, and 12:

```
BENEFICIAL OWNER REQUIREMENT
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

PURPOSE:
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ Prevents treaty shopping through conduit arrangements          │
│                                                                 │
│ WITHOUT beneficial owner test:                                  │
│ • Third country investor interposes resident of CS             │
│ • Conduit receives dividend and passes to third country        │
│ • Conduit claims reduced WHT rate                               │
│ • Third country investor gets treaty benefit indirectly        │
│                                                                 │
│ WITH beneficial owner test:                                     │
│ • Conduit is NOT beneficial owner                               │
│ • Reduced rate DENIED                                           │
│ • Full domestic WHT applies                                     │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

WHAT IS "BENEFICIAL OWNER"?
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ NOT A FORMAL LEGAL OWNER TEST                                   │
│                                                                 │
│ Commentary guidance (post-2014):                                │
│ • Must have "full right to use and enjoy" the dividend         │
│ • Not acting as agent, nominee, or conduit                     │
│ • Not under contractual/legal obligation to pass on            │
│ • Substance over form approach                                  │
│                                                                 │
│ CONDUIT = NOT beneficial owner                                  │
│ AGENT/NOMINEE = NOT beneficial owner                            │
│ GENUINE INVESTOR = beneficial owner                             │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
```

### 3.4 The 365-Day Holding Period (2017 Addition)

The 2017 OECD MTC added a **minimum holding period**:

```
365-DAY HOLDING REQUIREMENT
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

ARTICLE 10(2)(a) REQUIRES:
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ Holding of ≥25% "throughout a 365 day period that includes     │
│ the day of the payment of the dividend"                         │
│                                                                 │
│ PURPOSE:                                                        │
│ → Prevents "dividend stripping" transactions                   │
│ → Acquirer buys shares shortly before dividend                 │
│ → Claims reduced 5% rate                                        │
│ → Sells shares shortly after dividend                          │
│                                                                 │
│ NOTE: Some countries have reserved the right NOT to include    │
│ this holding period (e.g., Luxembourg Reservation [77.1])      │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
```

## 5. ARTICLE 10(4): PE EXCEPTION

### 5.1 Effectively Connected Dividends

```
PE EXCEPTION - ARTICLE 10(4)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

RULE:
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ If dividends are paid in respect of holdings EFFECTIVELY       │
│ CONNECTED with a PE in the other state:                         │
│                                                                 │
│ → Article 10(1) and (2) do NOT apply                           │
│ → Article 7 (Business Profits) applies instead                 │
│ → No WHT limitation; full PE taxation rules apply              │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

"EFFECTIVELY CONNECTED" REQUIRES:
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ MORE than merely recording shareholding in PE's books          │
│                                                                 │
│ Must show genuine business connection:                          │
│ • Shareholding held for PE's business purposes                 │
│ • PE manages/administers the investment                        │
│ • Economic nexus between shares and PE activities              │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

UN MODEL EXTENSION:
→ UN also extends to "fixed base" (for independent personal
   services under UN Article 14)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

## 6. ARTICLE 10(5): EXTRATERRITORIAL TAXATION PROHIBITION

### 6.1 Two Prohibitions

```
EXTRATERRITORIAL TAXATION PROHIBITION
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

PROHIBITION 1: No taxation solely based on profit origin
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ A state CANNOT tax dividends distributed by a non-resident     │
│ company solely because the corporate profits from which        │
│ distributions are made originated in its territory.            │
│                                                                 │
│ ALLOWED: Tax if paid to resident OR to PE in that state        │
│ NOT ALLOWED: Tax just because profits originated there         │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

PROHIBITION 2: No special undistributed profits tax
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ A state CANNOT subject non-resident companies to special       │
│ taxes on undistributed profits.                                 │
│                                                                 │
│ NOTE: Does NOT affect residence state taxation, including      │
│       CFC rules                                                 │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

An important technical difference (June 2022 exam point):

```
"SUCH DIVIDENDS" - UN vs OECD
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

### 10.1 Credit vs Exemption Method

```
DTR METHODS FOR DIVIDENDS

CREDIT METHOD (Article 23B):
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ Residence state taxes worldwide income                          │
│ Credit given for source state WHT                               │
│                                                                 │
│ ORDINARY CREDIT: Credit limited to residence state tax on      │
│                  foreign income (no excess refund)              │
│                                                                 │

└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

EXEMPTION METHOD (Article 23A):
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ Residence state exempts foreign dividend income                 │
│                                                                 │
│ BUT: Commentary on Article 23B, [58] notes that exemption      │
│      countries may still use credit for limited WHT (5%/15%)   │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

DEVELOPING COUNTRY CONSIDERATION:
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ Exemption method may be preferable because:                     │
│ • Source state can freely set tax rates                        │
│ • Can offer incentives without residence state capturing       │
│   benefit through reduced credit                                │
│ (Garfias von Furstenberg, 2021)                                │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

## 11. DEVELOPING COUNTRY PERSPECTIVES

### 11.1 The Reciprocity Problem

**EXAM CRITICAL** (June 2022 Q4 specific focus):

```
DEVELOPING COUNTRY CRITIQUE OF ARTICLE 10
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

THE RECIPROCITY ASSUMPTION:
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ OECD MTC assumes RECIPROCITY between contracting states        │
│                                                                 │
│ BUT: Reciprocity requires parties capable of providing and     │
│      forsaking broadly same level of resources                  │
│                                                                 │
│ CAPITAL IMPORTING vs CAPITAL EXPORTING:                         │
│ • Developing countries: typically CAPITAL IMPORTERS            │
│ • Developed countries: typically CAPITAL EXPORTERS             │
│                                                                 │
│ IMBALANCE:                                                      │
│ • Dividends flow predominantly FROM developing countries       │
│ • WHT limitations benefit residence (developed) countries      │
│ • Revenue transferred from source to residence                  │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

INTERESTS NOT SUFFICIENTLY CONSIDERED:
→ BEPS Actions did not formally focus on this imbalance
→ Implicitly contained but not directly addressed (Brauner 2014)
→ Recent changes have not adequately considered capital importers
   (Garfias von Furstenberg, 2021)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
```

### 11.2 Suggestions for Reform

```
REFORM SUGGESTIONS FOR DEVELOPING COUNTRIES
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

OPTION 1: NOT ENTERING DTAs
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ Rely on domestic law alone for:                                 │
│ • Source taxation (no limitations)                              │
│ • Unilateral double tax relief                                  │
│                                                                 │
│ ADVANTAGE: Full control over dividend taxation                  │
│ DISADVANTAGE: May deter foreign investment                      │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

OPTION 2: EXEMPTION METHOD IN RESIDENCE STATE
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ Include Article 10 but require exemption (not credit) method   │
│                                                                 │
│ EFFECT: Source state freely determines tax rate/incentives     │
│         Residence state does not "capture" foregone source tax │
