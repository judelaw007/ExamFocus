## 1. INTRODUCTION: THE ROLE OF ARTICLE 19

### 1.1 Purpose and Function

Article 19 provides **special rules** for the taxation of remuneration (salaries and pensions) paid by a Contracting State, its political subdivisions, or local authorities in respect of **government service**. The article represents a significant departure from the normal rules applicable to employment income (Article 15) and private pensions (Article 18).

### 1.2 Policy Rationale

```
WHY SPECIAL RULES FOR GOVERNMENT SERVICE?
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

UNDERLYING PRINCIPLES:
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ 1. SOVEREIGN IMMUNITY                                           │
│    • States traditionally do not tax each other                │
│    • Extension to state employees reflects comity              │
│    • Prevents host state from taxing foreign diplomats/staff   │
│                                                                 │
│ 2. LOYALTY TO PAYING STATE                                      │
│    • Government employees serve their state                    │
│    • Paying state has greater connection to the income         │
│    • Tax allegiance follows service allegiance                 │
│                                                                 │
│ 3. ADMINISTRATIVE CONVENIENCE                                   │
│    • Paying state already administers payroll                  │
│    • Avoids complexity of host state taxation                  │
│    • Single point of tax administration                         │
│                                                                 │
│ 4. RECIPROCITY                                                  │
│    • All states have government employees abroad               │
│    • Mutual benefit from exclusive source state rule           │
│    • Balanced allocation of taxing rights                       │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
```

### 1.3 Historical Development

- **1963 Draft Convention**: Original text referred to services "in the discharge of functions of a governmental nature"
- **1977 Model Convention**: Restructured to current format
- **2005 Update**: Paragraph 1 amended to delete "other than a pension" in subparagraph (a), clarifying the scope of paragraph 2 for pensions

### 1.4 Relationship with Other Articles

Article 19 is a **lex specialis** that takes priority over general provisions:

| General Rule | Article 19 Override |
|--------------|---------------------|
| **Article 15** | Employment income | Article 19(1) for government salaries |
| **Article 16** | Directors' fees | Article 19(3) - business exception |
| **Article 17** | Entertainers/sportspersons | Article 19(3) - business exception |
| **Article 18** | Pensions | Article 19(2) for government pensions |

## 2. ARTICLE 19 TEXT AND STRUCTURE (OECD MTC 2017)

### 2.1 Full Text

**Article 19(1) - Salaries and Wages:**

> "(a) Salaries, wages and other similar remuneration paid by a Contracting State or a political subdivision or a local authority thereof to an individual in respect of services rendered to that State or subdivision or authority shall be taxable only in that State.
>
> (b) However, such salaries, wages and other similar remuneration shall be taxable only in the other Contracting State if the services are rendered in that State and the individual is a resident of that State who:
> (i) is a national of that State; or
> (ii) did not become a resident of that State solely for the purpose of rendering the services."

**Article 19(2) - Pensions:**

> "(a) Notwithstanding the provisions of paragraph 2 of Article 18, pensions and other similar remuneration paid by, or out of funds created by, a Contracting State or a political subdivision or a local authority thereof to an individual in respect of services rendered to that State or subdivision or authority shall be taxable only in that State.
>
> (b) However, such pensions and other similar remuneration shall be taxable only in the other Contracting State if the individual is a resident of, and a national of, that State."

**Article 19(3) - Business Activities Exception:**

> "The provisions of Articles 15, 16, 17 and 18 shall apply to salaries, wages, pensions and other similar remuneration in respect of services rendered in connection with a business carried on by a Contracting State or a political subdivision or a local authority thereof."

## 3. ARTICLE 19(1): GOVERNMENT SALARIES

### 3.1 Basic Rule - Paragraph 1(a)

The **paying state** has the **exclusive** right to tax salaries, wages, and similar remuneration paid for government services:

```
ARTICLE 19(1)(a) - PAYING STATE TAXATION
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

KEY ELEMENTS:
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ 1. PAYER QUALIFICATION                                          │
│    → Contracting State itself                                   │
│    → Political subdivision (e.g., states, provinces, regions)  │
│    → Local authority (e.g., municipalities, councils)          │
│                                                                 │
│ 2. TYPE OF PAYMENT                                              │
│    → Salaries                                                   │
│    → Wages                                                      │
│    → Other similar remuneration                                 │
│                                                                 │
│ 3. NEXUS TO GOVERNMENT SERVICE                                  │
│    → "in respect of services rendered to" the state            │
│    → Governmental functions (not commercial activities)         │
│                                                                 │
│ 4. TAXING RIGHT                                                 │
│    → "shall be taxable only in that State"                     │
│    → EXCLUSIVE to paying state                                  │
│    → Other state (even if residence state) must exempt         │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
```

### 3.2 The Nationality Exception - Paragraph 1(b)

Article 19(1)(b) provides an **exception** to the paying state rule:

```
NATIONALITY EXCEPTION - ARTICLE 19(1)(b)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

The OTHER STATE (not the paying state) may tax if ALL of:
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ CONDITION 1: Services rendered in that other state              │
│              (physical presence during work)                    │
│                                                                 │
│ CONDITION 2: Individual is RESIDENT of that other state         │
│              (under Article 4)                                  │
│                                                                 │
│ CONDITION 3: EITHER:                                            │
│   (i)  Individual is NATIONAL of that other state               │
│   OR                                                            │
│   (ii) Individual did NOT become resident solely for the        │
│        purpose of rendering the services                        │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

RATIONALE:
→ Protects local nationals working for foreign governments
→ Protects persons who were already residents before taking
  government employment
→ Prevents paying state from exclusively taxing income of
  persons with strong ties to another state
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
```


### 3.4 "Solely for the Purpose of Rendering Services"

This limb of the exception (Article 19(1)(b)(ii)) is critical:

```
"SOLELY FOR THE PURPOSE" TEST
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

INTERPRETATION:
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ The question is: Why did the individual become resident?        │
│                                                                 │
│ IF the individual became resident SOLELY to render the          │
│ government services:                                            │
│ → Exception does NOT apply                                      │
│ → Paying state has exclusive right                              │
│                                                                 │
│ IF the individual was ALREADY resident, OR became resident      │
│ for OTHER REASONS (family, personal choice, etc.):             │
│ → Exception APPLIES                                             │
│ → Host state may tax                                            │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

COMMENTARY GUIDANCE:
→ This test protects persons with genuine connections to the
  host state who happen to work for a foreign government
→ Prevents abuse where persons relocate solely to avoid
  host state taxation
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
```

## 4. ARTICLE 19(2): GOVERNMENT PENSIONS

### 4.1 Basic Rule - Paragraph 2(a)

Government pensions follow a similar pattern to government salaries:

```
ARTICLE 19(2)(a) - GOVERNMENT PENSIONS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

KEY ELEMENTS:
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ 1. PAYER/SOURCE                                                 │
│    → Paid BY a Contracting State/subdivision/local authority   │
│    → OR paid OUT OF FUNDS CREATED BY such entities             │
│                                                                 │
│ 2. NEXUS                                                        │
│    → "in respect of services rendered" to the state            │
│    → Must relate to past government service                    │
│                                                                 │
│ 3. TAXING RIGHT                                                 │
│    → "shall be taxable only in that State"                     │
│    → Paying state EXCLUSIVE                                     │
│    → Overrides Article 18 (private pensions)                   │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

NOTE: Article 19(2)(a) expressly states "Notwithstanding the
provisions of paragraph 2 of Article 18" - confirming priority
over the general pension rule.
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
```

### 4.2 The Nationality Exception - Paragraph 2(b)

For pensions, the exception is **narrower** than for salaries:

```
PENSION NATIONALITY EXCEPTION - ARTICLE 19(2)(b)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

The OTHER STATE may tax government pensions ONLY if:
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ CONDITION 1: Individual is RESIDENT of that other state         │
│              AND                                                │
│ CONDITION 2: Individual is NATIONAL of that other state         │
│                                                                 │
│ BOTH CONDITIONS MUST BE MET                                     │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

COMPARED TO SALARIES (Article 19(1)(b)):
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ SALARIES EXCEPTION requires:                                    │
│ - Services rendered in other state                              │
│ - Resident of other state                                       │
│ - National OR did not become resident solely for services      │
│                                                                 │
│ PENSIONS EXCEPTION requires:                                    │
│ - Resident of other state                                       │
│ - National of other state                                       │
│                                                                 │
│ KEY DIFFERENCES:                                                │
│ → No "place of services" requirement for pensions               │
│ → No "solely for the purpose" alternative for pensions         │
│ → BOTH residence AND nationality required for pensions         │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
```

## 5. ARTICLE 19(3): THE BUSINESS ACTIVITIES EXCEPTION

### 5.1 Overview

Article 19(3) provides that the **normal employment and pension rules apply** when services are rendered in connection with a **business** carried on by the state:

```
BUSINESS ACTIVITIES EXCEPTION - ARTICLE 19(3)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

WHEN ARTICLE 19 DOES NOT APPLY:
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ If services are rendered "in connection with a BUSINESS        │
│ carried on by" the state/subdivision/local authority:          │
│                                                                 │
│ → Article 19 does NOT apply                                     │
│ → Normal rules apply:                                           │
│   • Article 15 for employment income                            │
│   • Article 16 for directors' fees                              │
│   • Article 17 for entertainers/sportspersons                  │
│   • Article 18 for pensions                                     │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

RATIONALE:
→ Article 19 is for GOVERNMENTAL functions
→ When the state acts commercially, it should be treated like
  any other employer
→ Prevents unfair advantage for state-owned businesses
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
```

### 5.2 What Constitutes "Business"?

```
GOVERNMENTAL vs BUSINESS ACTIVITIES
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

ARTICLE 19 APPLIES (Governmental Functions):
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ • Diplomatic and consular services                              │
│ • Military personnel                                            │
│ • Civil servants in government departments                      │
│ • Judges and judicial staff                                     │
│ • Police and law enforcement                                    │
│ • Public administration                                         │
│ • Teachers in public schools (typically)                        │
│ • Healthcare workers in public hospitals (often)               │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

ARTICLE 19 DOES NOT APPLY (Business Activities):
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ • State-owned commercial enterprises                            │
│ • National airlines (state-owned)                               │
│ • State-owned banks                                             │
│ • State-owned manufacturing companies                           │
│ • Public utilities operated commercially                        │
│ • State broadcasting corporations (may depend on structure)    │
│ • State tourism boards (commercial activities)                 │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

GREY AREAS (require case-by-case analysis):
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ • Central bank employees                                        │
│ • Port authorities                                              │
│ • Public universities (teaching vs research contracts)         │
│ • Public hospitals (public health vs fee-paying services)      │
│ • Cultural institutions (museums, orchestras)                  │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
```

### 5.3 Commentary Guidance

**Commentary [5]-[6]** provides that:

- The distinction depends on the **nature of the services** rendered
- Services of a **governmental nature** fall within Article 19
- Services in connection with **commercial/industrial activities** fall outside
- The fact that the employer is the state does not automatically bring the income within Article 19

```

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
```


### 6.3 Nationality Definition

Nationality for Article 19 purposes is determined by **Article 3(1)(g)**:

> "The term 'national', in relation to a Contracting State, means:
> (i) any individual possessing the nationality or citizenship of that Contracting State..."

### 9.3 Saving Clause Exception

Article 19 is **excepted** from the saving clause (Article 1(3)):

```

Article 1(3) saving clause does NOT apply to Article 19

This means:
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ A Contracting State cannot use the saving clause to tax        │
│ its own residents on government service income that falls      │
│ within Article 19.                                              │
│                                                                 │
│ EXAMPLE:                                                        │
│ Country Y resident works for Country X government in Country Y │
│ If Article 19(1)(a) applies (paying state rule), Country Y     │
│ cannot invoke the saving clause to tax this income.            │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
```

## 10. SPECIAL CASES

### 10.1 Diplomats and Consular Officials

Diplomatic and consular staff are typically covered by:
- **Vienna Convention on Diplomatic Relations (1961)**
- **Vienna Convention on Consular Relations (1963)**
- **Article 19** of tax treaties

```
DIPLOMATIC IMMUNITY AND ARTICLE 19

→ Diplomatic agents
→ Administrative and technical staff
→ Consular officers

Article 19 complements this by:
→ Allocating taxing right to SENDING state
→ Ensuring no gap in taxation
→ Providing treaty-based framework

EXAM NOTE: Know relationship between Vienna Conventions
and Article 19 - they work together, not in conflict
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
```

### 11.3 Sample Question Analysis

```
SAMPLE EXAM QUESTION
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Mr F is a national of Country X. He has been resident in
Country Y for 15 years (he moved there for family reasons).
He is employed by the Country X embassy in Country Y.

He also works part-time for a Country X state-owned airline
that operates commercial flights.

He receives:
- Embassy salary: $80,000
- Airline salary: $20,000

Analyse the taxation of Mr F's income.

ANALYSIS:
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ EMBASSY SALARY ($80,000):                                       │
│ → Article 19(1)(a): Paid by Country X for government services  │
│ → Basic rule: Taxable only in Country X                         │
│                                                                 │
│ → Check Article 19(1)(b) exception:                             │
│   • Services rendered in Country Y? YES                        │
│   • Mr F resident of Country Y? YES                             │
│   • Mr F national of Country Y? NO (national of X)             │
│   • Did he become resident solely for services? NO             │
│     (moved for family reasons 15 years ago)                    │
│                                                                 │
│ → Article 19(1)(b)(ii) exception applies                        │
│ → RESULT: Embassy salary taxable only in Country Y             │
│                                                                 │
│ AIRLINE SALARY ($20,000):                                        │
│ → Article 19(3): State-owned airline = business activity       │
```

### 12.3 Key Distinctions

```

1. ARTICLE 19 vs ARTICLE 15:
   → Government employment = Article 19
   → Private employment = Article 15
   → State business employment = Article 15 (via 19(3))

2. ARTICLE 19(2) vs ARTICLE 18:
   → Government pension = Article 19(2) (paying state)
   → Private pension = Article 18 (residence state)

3. SALARY vs PENSION EXCEPTIONS:
   → Salary: Nationality OR "not solely for services"
   → Pension: Nationality AND residence (stricter)

4. GOVERNMENT vs BUSINESS:
   → Governmental functions = Article 19 applies
   → Commercial activities = Article 19(3) exclusion
```
