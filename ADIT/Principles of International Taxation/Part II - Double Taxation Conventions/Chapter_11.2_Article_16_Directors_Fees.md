## 1. INTRODUCTION: THE ROLE OF ARTICLE 16

### 1.1 Purpose and Function

Article 16 allocates taxing rights over **directors' fees** - remuneration received by an individual in their capacity as a member of the board of directors of a company. It is one of the shortest and most stable provisions in the OECD Model, having remained virtually unchanged since 1963.

### 1.2 Key Characteristics

```
ARTICLE 16 - KEY FEATURES
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

1. LEX SPECIALIS TO ARTICLE 15
   Article 16 takes precedence over Article 15 (employment income)
   for remuneration received IN CAPACITY AS director

2. COMPANY RESIDENCE STATE TAXATION
   Directors' fees may be taxed in the state where the
   COMPANY is resident (not the individual)

3. PLACE OF ACTIVITY IRRELEVANT
   Unlike Article 15, taxing right does NOT depend on where
   the director performs duties

4. SIMPLE RULE
   One-sentence provision (OECD)
   → No thresholds, no conditions, no exceptions

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
```

### 1.3 Rationale

The company residence state is permitted to tax because:
- Directors bear fiduciary responsibility to the company
- Directors participate in company's strategic decisions
- Close connection between director role and company's residence state
- Difficult to determine where director functions are actually exercised

---

## 2. ARTICLE 16 TEXT (OECD MTC 2017)

### 2.1 Full Text

**OECD Model Article 16:**
> "Directors' fees and other similar payments derived by a resident of a Contracting State in his capacity as a member of the board of directors of a company which is a resident of the other Contracting State may be taxed in that other State."

### 2.2 Structure Analysis

| Element | Content |
|---------|---------|
| **Income covered** | Directors' fees and other similar payments |
| **Recipient** | Resident of a Contracting State |
| **Capacity** | As member of board of directors |
| **Payer** | Company resident of OTHER Contracting State |
| **Taxing right** | May be taxed in company's residence state |

---

## 3. KEY ELEMENTS OF ARTICLE 16

### 3.1 "Directors' Fees and Other Similar Payments"

```
SCOPE OF PAYMENTS UNDER ARTICLE 16
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

INCLUDED:
• Directors' fees (fixed or variable)
• Board attendance fees
• Other similar payments derived in capacity as director
• Benefits in kind (per Commentary):
  - Stock options
  - Use of residence or vehicle
  - Health or life insurance coverage
  - Club memberships

NOT INCLUDED:
• Salaries for day-to-day management activities → Article 15
• Pension payments to former directors → Article 18
• Remuneration for services not in director capacity → Article 15

KEY QUESTION:
Is the payment derived "in capacity as" member of board?
→ Yes: Article 16
→ No: Article 15 (or other applicable article)

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
```

### 3.2 "In His Capacity as a Member of the Board of Directors"

This is the **critical requirement** - the payment must be for services performed AS a director, not for other roles.

**Commentary guidance:**
- Payment must be for board membership role
- Where individual is both director AND employee (e.g., managing director), allocate between:
  - Director functions → Article 16
  - Employee functions → Article 15

### 3.3 "Board of Directors"

The term "board of directors" is not defined in the Model Convention. The Commentary provides:

```
BOARD OF DIRECTORS - INTERPRETATION
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

VARIES BY JURISDICTION:
• One-tier systems: Single board of directors
• Two-tier systems: Supervisory board + management board

COMMENTARY GUIDANCE:
Article 16 covers remuneration to members of organs that are
competent for "general supervision over the management of the
company"

TWO-TIER SYSTEMS:
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ SUPERVISORY BOARD (Aufsichtsrat)                               │
│ • Oversees management                                          │
│ • No day-to-day management role                                │
│ • Members: "directors" for Article 16                          │
│                                                                 │
│ MANAGEMENT BOARD (Vorstand)                                    │
│ • Day-to-day management                                        │
│ • Members may be "employees" for Article 15                    │
│ • Depends on domestic law characterisation                     │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
```

### 3.4 "May Be Taxed in That Other State"

"May be taxed" indicates:
- Company's residence state has a **right** (not obligation) to tax
- Individual's residence state **also** has taxing rights
- Creates **shared taxing rights** → Double taxation relief under Article 23

## 4. RELATIONSHIP WITH ARTICLE 15

### 4.1 Lex Specialis

Article 16 is a **special provision** that takes precedence over Article 15 for remuneration received in the capacity of a director.

**From June 2023 Exam:**
> "As noted above there is a need to first consider whether the income derived by Mr Ky could fall within a different (more specific distributive) DTA provision (e.g., under Articles 16-19 as 'lex specialis')."

### 4.2 Distinguishing Article 16 from Article 15

```
ARTICLE 16 vs ARTICLE 15
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

                    ARTICLE 16              ARTICLE 15
─────────────────   ──────────────────      ──────────────────────
ROLE:               Board member            Employee
CAPACITY:           Director                Employment relationship
SOURCE NEXUS:       Company residence       Where work performed
183-DAY RULE:       Does NOT apply          May apply (15(2))
TAXING RIGHT:       Company's state         Place of work
PLACE MATTERS:      NO                      YES

COMMON SCENARIO - MANAGING DIRECTOR:
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ Individual is both:                                             │
│ • Director (board member) - Receives director fees              │
│ • Managing director (employee) - Receives salary                │
│                                                                 │
│ RESULT:                                                         │
│ • Director fees → Article 16 (company's state)                 │
│ • Salary → Article 15 (place of work/183-day rule)             │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
```

## 5. UN MODEL DIFFERENCES

### 5.1 Key Distinction: Top-Level Managerial Positions

The UN Model Article 16 includes a **second paragraph** that extends the scope beyond directors to **top-level managerial officials**.

### 5.2 UN Model Article 16 Text

**UN Model Article 16(1):**
> "Directors' fees and other similar payments derived by a resident of a Contracting State in his capacity as a member of the board of directors of a company which is a resident of the other Contracting State may be taxed in that other State."

**UN Model Article 16(2):**
> "Salaries, wages and other similar remuneration derived by a resident of a Contracting State in his capacity as an official in a **top-level managerial position** of a company which is a resident of the other Contracting State may be taxed in that other State."

### 5.3 "Top-Level Managerial Position"

```
UN MODEL ARTICLE 16(2) - TOP-LEVEL MANAGERS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

DEFINITION (UN Commentary):
"The term 'top-level managerial position' refers to a LIMITED
GROUP of positions that involve PRIMARY RESPONSIBILITY for the
general direction of the affairs of the company, apart from the
activities of the directors."

CHARACTERISTICS:
• Limited to senior executive positions
• Primary responsibility for company direction
• DISTINCT from board of directors role
• Not rank-and-file management

EXAMPLES:
• Chief Executive Officer (CEO)
• Chief Financial Officer (CFO)
• Chief Operating Officer (COO)
• Other C-suite executives

RATIONALE FOR UN EXTENSION:
• Developing countries want source taxation over executive pay
• Prevents high-level executives avoiding local taxation
• Recognises economic contribution of executives to source state

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
```

### 5.4 Comparison Table

| Feature | OECD Model | UN Model |
|---------|------------|----------|
| **Paragraphs** | 1 | 2 |
| **Directors** | Yes | Yes (para 1) |
| **Top-level managers** | No | Yes (para 2) |
| **Company state taxation** | Directors only | Directors AND top managers |
| **Scope** | Narrower | Broader source taxation |

## 6. INTERPRETIVE ISSUES

### 6.1 Who is a "Director"?

The Commentary acknowledges interpretation challenges:
- Who qualifies as a director?
- What activities are performed by a director?
- Which company organs are comprised?
- What conditions exist for board membership?

### 6.2 Domestic Law Reference

Under Article 3(2), undefined terms may be interpreted according to domestic law. This can create:
- Different scope in different treaties
- Potential mismatches between states
- Need for competent authority guidance

### 6.3 Legal Persons as Directors

Article 16 can apply where a **legal person** (company) serves as director:
- Director fees paid to corporate director
- Article 16 applies to the legal person resident of other state

## 7. EXAM APPLICATION

### 7.1 How Article 16 Appears in Exams

Article 16 typically appears:
1. **In elimination questions** - Ruling out Article 16 before applying Article 15
2. **In comparison questions** - OECD vs UN Model differences
3. **In mixed-role scenarios** - Individual is both director and employee

### 7.2 Answer Framework

```
ARTICLE 16 EXAM FRAMEWORK
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

1. IDENTIFY THE INCOME (2 marks)
   "The payment is [directors' fees / salary / other]..."
   - If directors' fees → Consider Article 16
   - If salary/wages → Likely Article 15

2. DETERMINE CAPACITY (2-3 marks)
   "The payment is derived in capacity as..."
   - Board member → Article 16
   - Employee/manager → Article 15
   - Mixed role → Allocate between articles

3. CHECK COMPANY RESIDENCE (2 marks)
   "The company is resident of State X under Article 4..."
   - Company must be resident of OTHER state (not individual's)

4. APPLY ARTICLE 16 (2-3 marks)
   "Under Article 16, the directors' fees MAY BE TAXED in
    State X (company's residence state)..."
   - Individual's state also has taxing rights
   - Relief under Article 23

5. UN MODEL DIFFERENCE (if relevant) (2 marks)
   "Under the UN Model, Article 16(2) extends to top-level
    managerial positions..."

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
```

### 7.3 Common Exam Pitfalls

| Pitfall | Correct Approach |
|---------|------------------|
| Assuming all director pay is Article 16 | Only in CAPACITY AS director |
| Applying 183-day rule to directors | Article 15(2) does NOT apply to Article 16 |
| Missing UN Model extension | UN includes top-level managers |
| Forgetting lex specialis | Check Article 16 BEFORE Article 15 |

   • Benefits in kind (stock options, car, etc.)
   • Only payments IN CAPACITY AS director

   • Article 16 takes precedence for director remuneration

   • CEO, CFO, COO and similar C-suite executives

   • Does NOT apply to Article 16
   • No conditions or thresholds
   • Simple: Company residence state may tax
