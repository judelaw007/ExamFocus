## 1. INTRODUCTION: THE ROLE OF ARTICLE 6

### 1.1 Purpose and Function

Article 6 allocates taxing rights over **income from immovable property** (real estate). It establishes the fundamental principle that the **situs state** (where the property is located) has the right to tax such income, reflecting the strong economic connection between immovable property and its location.

### 1.2 Policy Rationale

```
WHY SITUS STATE TAXATION FOR IMMOVABLE PROPERTY?
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

UNDERLYING PRINCIPLES:
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ 1. ECONOMIC NEXUS                                               │
│    • Immovable property has direct economic relationship        │
│      with the state where it is situated                       │
│    • Income derives from use of local resources/land           │
│    • State provides infrastructure, legal protection           │
│                                                                 │
│ 2. ABILITY TO ENFORCE                                           │
│    • Property cannot be moved to avoid taxation                │
│    • Situs state can effectively enforce tax claims            │
│    • Clear, administrable rule                                  │
│                                                                 │
│ 3. INTERNATIONAL CONSENSUS                                      │
│    • Most accepted source rule in international taxation       │
│    • Situs taxation predates modern treaty networks            │
│    • Reflects customary international tax practice             │
│                                                                 │
│ 4. BENEFIT PRINCIPLE                                            │
│    • Property owner benefits from local services               │
│    • Fair that local state receives tax revenue                │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
```

### 1.3 Historical Development

- **Mexico Draft (1943)** and **London Draft (1946)**: Situs state had **exclusive** taxing right
- **1959 OEEC**: Changed to "open" rule (both states may tax)
- **1963 OECD MTC**: Adopted current structure
- **Post-1963**: Structure and textual details have remained essentially unchanged (Reimer, 2022)

### 1.4 Nature of Article 6: The "Open" Rule

**EXAM CRITICAL**: Article 6 is an **"open" distributive rule**:

```
"OPEN" vs "CLOSED" DISTRIBUTIVE RULES
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

ARTICLE 6 = "OPEN" RULE:
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ • Situs state "MAY" tax → has the RIGHT but not obligation     │
│ • Residence state also retains right to tax                    │
│ • Both states may tax the same income                          │
│ • Residence state must provide DTR (exemption or credit)       │
│                                                                 │
│ Language: "may be taxed in that other State"                   │
│ Effect: Shared allocation, not exclusive                        │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

CONTRAST WITH "CLOSED" RULES:
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ Example: "shall be taxable ONLY in that State"                  │
│ → One state has EXCLUSIVE right                                 │
│ → Other state must exempt                                       │
│ → E.g., Article 8 (shipping/aircraft), Article 18 (pensions)   │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

December 2023 exam noted: Article 6 "leaves open both the residence
(Fragonia) and situs state (Geronia) to tax the relevant income"
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
```

## 2. ARTICLE 6 TEXT AND STRUCTURE (OECD MTC 2017)

### 2.1 Full Text

**Article 6(1):**
> "Income derived by a resident of a Contracting State from immovable property (including income from agriculture or forestry) situated in the other Contracting State may be taxed in that other State."

**Article 6(2):**
> "The term 'immovable property' shall have the meaning which it has under the law of the Contracting State in which the property in question is situated. The term shall in any case include property accessory to immovable property, livestock and equipment used in agriculture and forestry, rights to which the provisions of general law respecting landed property apply, usufruct of immovable property and rights to variable or fixed payments as consideration for the working of, or the right to work, mineral deposits, sources and other natural resources; ships, boats and aircraft shall not be regarded as immovable property."

**Article 6(3):**
> "The provisions of paragraph 1 shall apply to income derived from the direct use, letting, or use in any other form of immovable property."

**Article 6(4):**
> "The provisions of paragraphs 1 and 3 shall also apply to the income from immovable property of an enterprise."

┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ PARAGRAPH 1: BASIC RULE                                         │

## 3. DEFINITION OF "IMMOVABLE PROPERTY"

### 3.1 Dual Definition Approach

Article 6(2) uses a **dual approach** to define immovable property:

```
DEFINITION OF "IMMOVABLE PROPERTY"
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

1. PRIMARY DEFINITION: DOMESTIC LAW OF SITUS STATE
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ "shall have the meaning which it has under the law of the      │
│ Contracting State in which the property in question is         │
│ situated"                                                       │
│                                                                 │
│ → Domestic law of situs state is DETERMINATIVE                 │
│ → Not the residence state's law                                │
│ → Provides certainty (apply local law)                         │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

2. MINIMUM INCLUSIONS: "IN ANY CASE" INCLUDES:
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ • Property accessory to immovable property                     │
│ • Livestock and equipment used in agriculture and forestry     │
│ • Rights under general law respecting landed property          │
│ • Usufruct of immovable property                                │
│ • Rights to payments for working mineral deposits, sources,    │
│   and other natural resources                                   │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

3. EXPRESS EXCLUSION:
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ "ships, boats and aircraft shall NOT be regarded as            │
│ immovable property"                                             │
│                                                                 │
│ → Even if domestic law treats them as immovable               │
│ → Covered by separate provisions (Article 8, 22(3))           │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
```

### 3.3 December 2023 Exam Application

In the December 2023 exam (Angelica case), the warehouse was clearly immovable property:
- Geronia's domestic law defined immovable property as "land and all buildings on the land as well as any fixtures"
- The warehouse fell within this definition
- Article 6(2) reference to domestic law of situs state (Geronia) was applied

## 5. ARTICLE 6(4): ENTERPRISE PROPERTY

### 5.1 Purpose

Article 6(4) ensures that Article 6 applies to income from immovable property held by an **enterprise**:

```
ARTICLE 6(4) - ENTERPRISE PROPERTY
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

TEXT: "The provisions of paragraphs 1 and 3 shall also apply to
      the income from immovable property of an enterprise."

SIGNIFICANCE:
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ 1. CONFIRMS ARTICLE 6 PRIORITY OVER ARTICLE 7                   │
│    • Even if property is business asset                        │
│    • Even if enterprise has PE in situs state                  │
│    • Income from immovable property = Article 6, not Article 7 │
│                                                                 │
│ 2. SITUS STATE TAXING RIGHT WITHOUT PE                          │
│    • Enterprise can generate Article 6 income without PE       │
│    • Situs state taxes even if no PE threshold met            │
│    • Commentary on Article 6, [4]                               │
│                                                                 │
│ 3. ENTERPRISE vs INDIVIDUAL                                     │
│    • Article 6 applies to BOTH                                  │
│    • No distinction in treatment                                │
│    • Enterprise rental income = same as individual rental      │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
```

### 5.2 December 2023 Exam Application

In Angelica case:
- Angelica Co. was an enterprise (company)
- Warehouse was immovable property of that enterprise
- Article 6(4) confirmed that Article 6(1) and (3) applied to rental income
- Geronia (situs state) had taxing right under Article 6, regardless of whether Angelica had a PE in Geronia

## 6. PRIORITY AND INTERACTION WITH OTHER ARTICLES

### 6.1 Article 6 Priority Rule

**EXAM CRITICAL**: Article 6 takes **priority** over most other distributive articles:

```
ARTICLE 6 PRIORITY HIERARCHY
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

ARTICLE 6 TAKES PRIORITY OVER:
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ • Article 7 (Business Profits)                                  │
│ • Article 8 (Shipping/Air Transport) - N/A ships excluded      │
│ • Article 10 (Dividends) - N/A different income type           │
│ • Article 11 (Interest) - BUT mortgage interest = Article 11   │
│ • Article 12 (Royalties) - BUT consider Article 6(2) mineral   │
│   rights                                                        │
│ • Articles 15-20 (Employment, etc.) - N/A different income     │
│ • Article 21 (Other Income)                                     │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

NO PRIORITY RELATIONSHIP WITH:
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ • Article 13 (Capital Gains) - different timing (alienation)   │
│ • Article 22 (Capital) - different tax type (wealth/capital)   │
│                                                                 │
│ Article 6: INCOME from USE of property                          │
│ Article 13: GAINS from ALIENATION of property                   │
│ Article 22: TAX ON CAPITAL/WEALTH value                        │
│                                                                 │
│ These operate in parallel, not in conflict                      │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
```

### 6.2 Interaction with Article 7 (Business Profits)

```
ARTICLE 6 vs ARTICLE 7
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

GENERAL RULE:
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ Article 6 PREVAILS over Article 7 for income from immovable    │
│ property                                                        │
│                                                                 │
│ • Even if enterprise has PE in situs state                     │
│ • Even if property is part of PE's assets                      │
│ • Article 6(4) confirms this                                    │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

WHEN ARTICLE 7 APPLIES:
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ • Business profits NOT derived from immovable property         │
│ • Income from sales of products (even if stored in property)   │
│ • December 2023: "income derived from the sale of products     │
│   assembled in Geronia may have fallen within Article 7 as     │
│   business profits rather than being income with the requisite │
│   nexus to immovable property"                                  │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

KEY DISTINCTION:
Article 6: Income from USE of property (rental, letting, etc.)
Article 7: Income from BUSINESS ACTIVITIES (even if using property)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
```

### 7.1 Key Commentary Paragraphs

IMPORTANT COMMENTARY PROVISIONS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

### 7.2 Net vs Gross Taxation

**Commentary [5]**: In taxing income from immovable property:
- The object should be taxation of **profits** rather than gross income
- Expenses incurred in earning the income should be taken into account

This is consistent with general taxation principles and allows deductions for:
- Maintenance costs
- Insurance
- Property taxes (where deductible)
- Interest on loans (subject to domestic law limitations)

## 8. RECENT CASE LAW

### 8.1 Royal Bank of Canada v HMRC (2023)

**EXAM RELEVANT**: This 2023 UK Court of Appeal case clarified Article 6 scope:

```
ROYAL BANK OF CANADA v HMRC [2023] EWCA Civ 695
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

FACTS:
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ Assigned rights to payment were claimed to be income from      │
│ immovable property                                              │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

HELD:
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ • Assigned rights to payment did NOT fall within Article 6     │
│ • Payments could not be said to have a SOURCE in the land     │
│ • Nor any INTEREST IN LAND                                      │
│ • As there was no PE, Article 7 operated to provide exclusive │
│   taxing rights to residence state                              │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

SIGNIFICANCE:
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ • Article 6 requires direct nexus with immovable property      │
│ • Mere connection to land is insufficient                       │
│ • Payment must derive FROM the land/property itself            │
│ • Assigned contractual rights ≠ income from immovable property │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

(Cited in December 2023 exam suggested solutions)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
```

### 8.2 German BFH Case (1996)

Referenced in December 2023 exam:

> "Currency gains generated from the letting of immovable property were considered to be income from immovable property and so were considered to fall within Article 6" (German BFH, 18 September 1996, BFH 1 R 69/95)

This supports the view that incidental income closely connected to letting may fall within Article 6.

### 12.1 Article 6 at a Glance

| Element | Rule |
|---------|------|
| **Income type** | From immovable property (direct use, letting, other) |
| **Taxing right** | Situs state MAY tax (open rule) |

### 12.2 Key Distinctions

CRITICAL EXAM DISTINCTIONS
